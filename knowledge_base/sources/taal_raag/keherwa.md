# Keherwa Taal

Keherwa is one of the most common taals (rhythmic cycles) in North Indian light
and devotional music. It has 8 matras (beats) arranged in two equal halves of 4
beats each (a 4 + 4 division).

## Structure

- Matras (beats): 8
- Vibhag (sections): 2, of 4 beats each
- Sam (the emphasised first beat): beat 1
- Khali (the "empty" / lightly-struck section): begins at beat 5

A simple theka (bol pattern) often written as:

  Dha Ge Na Ti  |  Na Ka Dhi Na

## Why it suits bhajans

Keherwa's steady, walking 8-beat cycle gives a gentle, swaying groove that sits
well under devotional singing. It supports both slow, emotional bhajans and
faster, celebratory ones simply by changing the tempo (laya):

- Slow (vilambit-ish) for tender aartis and reflective stuti.
- Medium for typical congregational bhajans.
- Fast (drut) for jaikara-style, high-energy Shyam bhajans and dhun.

Many Khatu Shyam bhajans, including aarti sung to the "Om Jai Jagdish Hare"
pattern, fit comfortably in Keherwa.
