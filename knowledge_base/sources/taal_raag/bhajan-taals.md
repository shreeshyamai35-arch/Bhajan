# Common Taals and Moods for Devotional Bhajans

Devotional bhajans use a small set of friendly, cyclical taals. Choosing the
taal and tempo (laya) sets the mood of a Shyam bhajan.

## Frequently used taals

- Keherwa (8 beats, 4+4): the default for most bhajans; gentle and versatile.
- Dadra (6 beats, 3+3): a lilting, swaying feel; lovely for tender aartis and
  lullaby-like devotional songs.
- Bhajani / Bhajan theka (an 8-beat folk-bhajan cycle): a rolling kirtan groove
  used widely in congregational singing.
- Roopak (7 beats, 3+2+2): less common, gives a slightly off-balance, soulful
  movement; used for more classical-leaning bhajans.

## Matching mood to tempo

- Slow / emotional (e.g. sandhya aarti, viraha bhajans): Keherwa or Dadra at a
  slow laya, soft tabla and harmonium, space for the voice.
- Devotional medium (typical Shyam bhajan): Keherwa at medium laya with steady
  dholak and manjira.
- Celebratory / jaikara (Nishan Yatra, festival): fast Keherwa or Bhajani with
  dholak, claps, and call-and-response chanting of "Jai Shri Shyam".

## Practical tips for Khatu Shyam bhajans

- Aartis sung to the "Om Jai Jagdish Hare" pattern sit naturally in Keherwa.
- Keep the sam (beat 1) strong so the congregation can clap and join.
- Build energy by raising the laya across repeats rather than changing the taal.
