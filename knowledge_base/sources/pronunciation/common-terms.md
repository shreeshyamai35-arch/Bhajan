# Pronunciation Guide — Khatu Shyam Devotional Terms

Hints below use simple English-syllable approximations. "aa" = long a as in
"father"; "ee" = long e as in "see"; stress the CAPS syllable.

## Names and places

- Shyam — "SHYAAM" (one syllable, long aa; soft sh). Not "shy-am".
- Khatu — "KHAA-too" (aspirated kh; long first vowel).
- Khatu Shyam — "KHAA-too SHYAAM".
- Barbarika — "bar-baa-REE-kaa" (also spelt Barbarik: "bar-baa-REEK").
- Shyam Baba — "SHYAAM baa-baa".
- Ghatotkacha — "gha-TOT-ka-cha".
- Krishna — "KRISH-na" (light, short final a).

## Worship terms

- darshan — "DUR-shun" (sacred viewing of the deity).
- aarti — "AAR-tee" (waving of lamps; song sung at that time).
- nishan — "ni-SHAAN" (the sacred flag carried to Khatu).
- prasad — "pra-SAAD" (blessed offering).
- bhajan — "BHU-jun" (devotional song).
- kirtan — "KEER-tun" (congregational call-and-response singing).
- dhun — "DHOON" (a repeated, chant-like melodic refrain).
- jaikara — "jai-KAA-raa" (a shouted cry of victory/praise).

## Common devotional phrases

- "Jai Shri Shyam" — "jai shree SHYAAM" (Victory to Shri Shyam).
- "Haare ka Sahara" — "HAA-re kaa sa-HAA-raa" (support of the defeated).
- "Teen Baan Dhari" — "TEEN baan DHAA-ree" (bearer of the three arrows).
- "Khatu Naresh" — "KHAA-too na-RESH" (Lord of Khatu).

## Singing tips

- Keep "Shyam" warm and open on the long aa; it is the emotional anchor word.
- Lengthen the final vowel of "Shyaam" and "Sahaaraa" on held notes.
- Crisp, light final short-a vowels (Krishna, bhajan) — do not over-stress them.
