# Shri Khatu Shyam Chalisa — Themes and Invocation

The Shyam Chalisa is a forty-verse (chalisa) hymn of praise to Khatu Shyam Ji,
sung like other chalisas in a steady, rolling devotional metre. It opens with a
doha (couplet) of salutation and then moves through verses recounting his birth,
his three arrows, his great sacrifice, and his grace toward devotees.

## Invocation (traditional opening sentiment)

Shri Shyam, Shyam — the devotee bows again and again,
remembering Barbarika, the giver of his own head,
the lord enshrined at Khatu, support of the helpless.

## Verses (paraphrased summary)

- Praise of his divine form, dark like a rain cloud, crowned with a peacock
  feather, holding the bow and the three infallible arrows.
- Remembrance of his lineage: son of Ghatotkacha, grandson of Bhima.
- The vow to aid the losing side, Krishna's test, and the gift of his head.
- The boon to watch the war and to be worshipped as Shyam in the age of Kali.
- The discovery of his head at Khatu and the rise of the great temple.
- Promises of protection, prosperity, and peace to those who remember him with
  faith; removal of sorrow, fear, and obstacles.

## Closing doha (sentiment)

Whoever recites this chalisa daily with devotion finds Shyam Baba ever near,
and the cry "Haare ka sahara" — refuge of the defeated — is answered.

Jai Shri Shyam. Khatu Naresh ki Jai.
