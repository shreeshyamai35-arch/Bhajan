# Shri Khatu Shyam Ji Ki Aarti — Om Jai Shri Shyam Hare

This is the principal aarti sung during the worship of Khatu Shyam Ji. It follows
the same melodic pattern as the well-known "Om Jai Jagdish Hare" aarti, so it fits
naturally with a slow, devotional bhajan tempo and is usually sung at the close of
darshan, morning (mangla) or evening (sandhya).

## Opening refrain (traditional)

Om Jai Shri Shyam Hare,
Baba Jai Shri Shyam Hare.
Khatu dham birajat,
Anupam roop dhare.
Om Jai Shri Shyam Hare.

## Devotional themes carried through the verses

The verses praise Shyam Baba seated at Khatu Dham in his matchless form. Devotees
describe waving the lamp before him, offering flowers and the sacred nishan
(flag), and singing his glory with cymbals and conch. He is invoked as the
compassionate one who lifts up the fallen and fulfils the wishes of the heart.

Common closing sentiment: whoever sings this aarti with love receives Shyam Baba's
grace, peace of mind, and the joy of darshan.

## Singing notes

- Mood: slow, tender, reverent — suited to Keherwa or Dadra taal.
- Call-and-response works well on the refrain "Om Jai Shri Shyam Hare".
- End on a sustained "Jai Shri Shyam" with the whole congregation.

Jai Shri Shyam.
